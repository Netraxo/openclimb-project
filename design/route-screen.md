# Route Screen

## Purpose

The Route Screen provides complete information about a climbing route.

It helps climbers decide whether the route matches their skills and expectations.

---

## User Goal

View all important route information in a clean and simple layout.

---

## Success

The user understands the route without unnecessary scrolling.

---

# Layout

## Header

- Route name
- Official grade
- Community grade
- Community rating

---

## Route Summary

Displays the most important information at a glance.

- Climbing type
- Route height
- Number of quickdraws
- Protection character
- Route character

Example

🏆 Jack Sparrow          VI.2

★★★★★

Community: VI.2+/3

Sport

📏 8 m

🪂 5 quickdraws

🙂 Cautious

Vertical • Arete • Roof

---

## Route Photo

Displays the main route photo.

Features:

- Fullscreen view
- Zoom
- Pan

Optional

Show route line

Displays the climbing line on the photo.

---

## Description

Short description of the route.

Example

Beautiful technical climbing on compact sandstone.

Excellent movement with a crux near the top.

---

## Warnings

Important information.

Examples

- Long sling recommended for the last quickdraw.
- Rock may be wet after rain.
- Loose block near the anchor.

---

## Route Information

Displays additional information.

- First ascent
- Author
- Year established

# Design Principles

- Keep the most important information at the top.
- Photos should support route identification.
- Community information never replaces official information.
- Official and community grades are always displayed separately.
- Comments should be short and useful.
- Minimal scrolling.
- Large touch targets.
- Clean and distraction-free interface.
